## 2. Executive synthesis — what the landscape means for OUR engine

**The market splits into three roles, and it validates building an orchestrator.**
- **Single-source providers** (Apollo, ZoomInfo, Cognism, Lusha, RocketReach, Seamless.AI, Lead411,
  UpLead, People Data Labs, FullContact, Hunter.io, Dropcontact) each own one proprietary dataset.
  Every one of them has a documented **regional/segment weakness** (Apollo & Lead411 strong US / weak
  international; Cognism & Dropcontact strong EU/UK; 6sense strong on intent / weak on contacts).
  These are **leaf sources to slot into a waterfall**, not orchestrators.
- **Waterfall orchestrators** — only **Clay** (150+ providers) and **BetterContact** (20+ providers)
  match what we are building: no owned database, chain providers per field-type until a valid hit,
  charge only on success. Clay reports coverage *tripling* vs any single source — the core value
  prop we must instrument (per-provider hit-rate, incremental lift, cost-per-match).
- **Intent/signal specialists** (Bombora, 6sense, Demandbase) are non-substitutable *input* signals
  (Bombora claims 86% exclusive data), run on their own account/domain-keyed, batch/async lane.

**Seven patterns recur across the field and become hard requirements for us:**
1. **Charge-on-success billing ("pay for what you find").** Near-universal (Apollo, RocketReach,
   UpLead, PDL, Hunter, Cognism redeem, Clay/BetterContact). → Cost (G4) must meter on provider
   *success*, not request count; reserve→reconcile with refund-on-miss where the provider doesn't
   bill misses. Distinguish *Data Credits* (provider cost, on-hit) from *orchestration compute*
   (Clay's model) so waterfall depth doesn't destroy margin.
2. **Search/preview → reveal.** Cheap/free discovery, paid reveal (ZoomInfo "search before enrich",
   Cognism preview→redeem, Lusha free preview→paid PII, Hunter cheap verify gating costly find). →
   Our orchestrator does cheap discovery + dedupe + cache-check **before** any paid reveal.
3. **Per-field, per-region, per-provider confidence drives ordering.** Every provider's stated
   strength is geographic/segment-specific. → Regional routing + a per-(provider, field, region)
   success/confidence table is mandatory (confirms `08`), and vendor accuracy claims must be
   replaced by *our measured* hit-rates.
4. **Quota/health via response headers + introspection.** PDL (`x-ratelimit-remaining`,
   `x-call-credits-spent`; 429 vs **402** = credits), Lusha/UpLead/Seamless/FullContact/Demandbase
   headers, Apollo `usage_stats`, ZoomInfo tiered rps. → Adapters ingest quota headers for adaptive
   throttling + budget gating, and must separate *rate-limit (retry)* from *credit-exhaustion
   (failover)*. Watch provider-specific quirks: **Hunter signals throttling with 403, not 429.**
5. **Async + signed webhooks for long/consent-gated calls.** Apollo (mandatory webhook for phone
   reveal), Clearbit/FullContact (HTTP 202 → webhook), Lusha (HMAC, 10s ack, retry≤3), Dropcontact
   (webhook **source-IP allow-list**). → Event-driven result collector + an **idempotent,
   HMAC-verified, SSRF-safe** webhook receiver (G2 + egress proxy `13`).
6. **Heterogeneous/small batch caps → a chunking layer.** Apollo bulk=10, 6sense=25, Lusha/PDL/
   BetterContact=100, Dropcontact=250, ZoomInfo async≈200K; **no bulk at all** for Lead411, Hunter,
   UpLead (fan-out per record). → A provider-aware batch/chunk + concurrency layer (`09`/`11`).
7. **Caching is mandatory to protect margin + honor terms.** Every credit-metered provider expects
   buyers to cache purchased records (ZoomInfo explicit; Cognism re-view free; Bombora ~7-day Surge
   TTL). → TTL-by-data-class cache (`16`), cross-tenant sharing only for non-PII (G1).

**Two cross-cutting risks the research surfaced:**
- **Provider supply continuity is real.** Clearbit's standalone Enrichment API is **sunset in 2026**
  (HubSpot-only) → we mark it `DEPRIORITIZED`; Seamless.AI had a **LinkedIn takedown (Mar 2025)**. →
  Never depend on a sole source; monitor provider continuity as a first-class health signal.
- **Compliance bar is high and includes data-broker registration.** ZoomInfo (SOC 2 Type II, ISO
  27001/27701, CA/VT data-broker registration) and Cognism (GDPR DNC/TPS suppression) set the floor.
  → Our compliance map (`18`) must add **data-broker registration, suppression/DNC lists, and
  per-record consent/provenance metadata**, not just SOC2/ISO/GDPR/CCPA checkboxes.
